Introduction to podcasts

A podcast is a digital audio program that people can listen to on demand, usually through apps like Spotify, Apple Podcasts, or YouTube Music. Podcasts are often released as episodes in a series and can cover almost any topic: education, storytelling, business, comedy, news, technology, health, and more.



A podcast is typically stored as a digital audio file, such as MP3, AAC, or WAV format, on a server or cloud-based hosting platform. The audio files are uploaded to a podcast hosting service, which generates an RSS feed containing information about the podcast and its episodes. Podcast directories and listening apps access this RSS feed to make episodes available to listeners. Storing podcasts online ensures easy distribution, streaming, and downloading across multiple devices while allowing creators to manage and update their content efficiently.
